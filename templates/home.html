Get Started

Before getting started, you can checkout the demo video below or browse through the public curricula to get an idea of what this application does.

Then all you need to do is:

  • Create an account
  • Create a curriculum
  • Create curriculum modules
  • Analyse your curriculum

Demo Video

Create an account

In order to create your own curricula and analyse your learning outcomes, you need to sign up for an account. This is because all your work is saved to a database so that you can come back and check it out anytime you wish. You will be able to update or delete your content at any time.

Create a curriculum

Once you have set up an account, sign in to start creating your own curricula. In the navigation bar at the top of the page you should now see a 'Curricula' section and a 'Taxonomies' section. To create a new curriculum, click on Curriculum:

Fill out the form and a new curriculum dashboard will be generated for you, which should look something like:

In our example, we named our curriculum 'Computer Science' and made it public by ticking the 'public' check-box in the form. If you didn't tick the check-box you should see a red button labeled 'private' instead. All public curricula may be browsed by other users. You can toggle between 'public' and 'private' by clicking on the button in the dashboard. The 'Update Curriculum' button brings you back to the original form where you can update any information and the 'Delete Curriculum' brings you to a new window asking you to confirm if you want to delete the curriculum from the database.

Create curriculum modules

In order to start analysing your learning outcomes, you need to create curriculum modules. Each module will contain a list of learning outcomes. To create a new module, click on Module in the 'modules' side-bar of your curriculum dashboard:

In the form, choose a title and colour for your module. Your module will appear in this colour in all charts. You'll be able to change it later on the fly in the side-bar and your charts will be update instantly. The important section of the form is the 'Learning Outcome List' field. Firstly, learning outcomes should be stated in a semantically coherent fashion. For instance, a suitable learning outcome would be "Students should be able to solve problems using skills of logic". This is in contrast to the bare form "able to solve problems using skills of logic", which is sometimes encountered and should not be used. Then, click on 'Create Module' to generate the new module. Repeat this step for all the modules in your curriculum and you should see each module added to your dashboard side-bar!

Analyse your curriculum

After adding your first module, you should now see a Analysis button appear at the top of your dashboard, next to the other buttons:

To create a new analysis, click on Analysis and choose a taxonomy for your analysis. Here you will have the option of any 'Admin' created taxonomy or your own custom taxonomy, which you can create here. You can also browse all public taxonomies.

Once you have chosen your taxonomy, click 'Start Analysis' to submit the form and wait for the analysis to finish. On completion, a link will be provided to you back to the dashboard and you should see all your analysis charts in the panel to the right. You can create as many analyses as you'd like, simply repeat the process, choose a different taxonomy and find a new analysis added to your dashboard! Note that updating your modules, by entering different learning outcomes for instance, doesn't update your analyses automatically. You will also have to update your analyses, using the 'Update Analysis' button.
